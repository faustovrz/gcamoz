1 Stelio’s Research Context & Experimental Setup

Based on Stelio’s correspondence, his doctoral research focuses on “Genetic Analysis of Tolerance to Low Phosphorus in Maize”. The breeding program is currently in its final phase, aiming to identify inbred lines with superior General Combining Ability (GCA) and Specific Combining Ability (SCA) under phosphorus-stressed conditions.

1.1 Germplasm & Selection Process

Stelio began his research with an extensive screening of environmental adaptation and “nicking” compatibility. The materials were selected as follows:

  • Low-Phosphorus Use Efficiency (PUE) Donors (Mexico/CIMMYT): Originally 21 lines were screened, and 7 promising PUE donor lines were selected for crossing: \[\text{CML364, CML366, CML439, CML440, CML434, CML435, and CML532}\]
  • Elite Local Inbred Lines (Mozambique): Originally over 50 lines were screened, and 7 elite Mozambican lines (susceptible to low-P but highly adapted to local agroecological conditions) were selected.

1.2 Evaluation Generation & Pedigree

  • Evaluation Generation: The genetic parameters (GCA and SCA) are being calculated on the \(F_1\) hybrid generation (\(N = 49\) hybrid families).
  • Future Populations: Concurrently, \(F_2\) populations are being developed from all 49 hybrid combinations. These \(F_2\) families are earmarked for subsequent genotyping, sequencing, and Quantitative Trait Loci (QTL) mapping to identify genomic regions associated with low-P tolerance.
  • Environments & Treatments: The \(F_1\) hybrids are evaluated under two phosphorus treatments (Low Phosphorus and Normal/High Phosphorus), which act as two distinct environments. The target trait is grain yield.

3 Linear Mixed Model Equations

To analyze these experiments, we must translate the physical design into statistical models. Below are the equations for both the Factorial Design (Stelio’s setup) and the Diallel Design (simulated training setup), under single and multi-environment scenarios.

3.1 Model 1: Factorial Mating (Line × Tester) Equations

In a Factorial mating design, the female and male lines are distinct. This means female general combining ability (\(GCA_F\)) and male general combining ability (\(GCA_M\)) are modeled as two separate, independent random effects with distinct variances.

3.1.1 Single-Environment Model

For an evaluation in a single environment (e.g., one phosphorus treatment), under a Randomized Complete Block Design (RCBD):

\[y_{jpqk} = \mu + B_j + F_p + M_q + (FM)_{pq} + \epsilon_{jpqk}\]

Where: * \(y_{jpqk}\) is the phenotype (yield) of the \(k\)-th plant of the cross between female \(p\) and male \(q\) in block \(j\). * \(\mu\) is the overall population mean. * \(B_j\) is the random effect of the \(j\)-th block, assumed \(B_j \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_B)\). * \(F_p\) is the random GCA effect of the \(p\)-th female parent, assumed \(F_p \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_F)\). * \(M_q\) is the random GCA effect of the \(q\)-th male parent, assumed \(M_q \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_M)\). * \((FM)_{pq}\) is the random SCA effect of the cross between female \(p\) and male \(q\), assumed \((FM)_{pq} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{FM})\). * \(\epsilon_{jpqk}\) is the residual experimental error, assumed \(\epsilon_{jpqk} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{\epsilon})\).

3.1.2 Multi-Environment Model (Including Phosphorus Treatments)

When evaluating the factorial cross across multiple environments (e.g., two phosphorus treatments: low-P and normal-P):

\[y_{ijpqk} = \mu + E_i + B_{j(i)} + F_p + M_q + (FM)_{pq} + (EF)_{ip} + (EM)_{iq} + (EFM)_{ipq} + \epsilon_{ijpqk}\]

Where: * \(E_i\) is the fixed effect of the \(i\)-th environment (phosphorus treatment). * \(B_{j(i)}\) is the random effect of the \(j\)-th block nested within the \(i\)-th environment, \(B_{j(i)} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{B(E)})\). * \((EF)_{ip}\) is the random interaction between the GCA of female \(p\) and environment \(i\), \((EF)_{ip} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{FE})\). * \((EM)_{iq}\) is the random interaction between the GCA of male \(q\) and environment \(i\), \((EM)_{iq} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{ME})\). * \((EFM)_{ipq}\) is the random interaction between the SCA of cross \(pq\) and environment \(i\) (SCA \(\times\) E), \((EFM)_{ipq} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{FME})\). * \(\epsilon_{ijpqk}\) is the residual error, \(\epsilon_{ijpqk} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{\epsilon})\).

3.2 Model 2: Diallel Mating (Overlaid Parents) Equations

In a Diallel mating design, the same parent can be used as a male or female. Therefore, the GCA effect of a parent must be identical whether it acts as the male or female. In mixed models, this is achieved by overlaying the design matrices of the female and male factors, resulting in a single GCA variance component (\(\sigma^2_{GCA}\)).

3.2.1 Single-Environment Model

For a diallel cross evaluated in one environment under RCBD:

\[y_{jklm} = \mu + B_j + g_k + g_l + s_{kl} + r_{kl} + \epsilon_{jklm}\]

Where: * \(y_{jklm}\) is the yield of the \(m\)-th individual in block \(j\) from the cross between parents \(k\) and \(l\). * \(g_k\) and \(g_l\) are the random GCA effects of parent \(k\) and parent \(l\), respectively. They are constrained to be identically distributed: \(g \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{GCA})\). The total additive genetic effect of the family is the sum of parental GCAs: \(g_k + g_l\). * \(s_{kl}\) is the random SCA effect of the cross between parents \(k\) and \(l\). It is symmetric (\(s_{kl} = s_{lk}\)), and \(s_{kl} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{SCA})\). * \(r_{kl}\) is the reciprocal effect (\(r_{kl} = -r_{lk}\)), modeling maternal or cytoplasmic effects. * \(\epsilon_{jklm}\) is the random residual error, \(\epsilon_{jklm} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{\epsilon})\).

3.2.2 Multi-Environment Model (Simulation Standard)

For the 2-environment simulated training dataset, we model the diallel as:

\[y_{ijklm} = \mu + E_i + B_{j(i)} + g_k + g_l + s_{kl} + (Eg)_{ik} + (Eg)_{il} + (Es)_{ikl} + \epsilon_{ijklm}\]

Where: * \(E_i\) is the fixed effect of environment \(i\) (\(i = \text{env1}, \text{env2}\)). * \(B_{j(i)}\) is the random block nested within environment, \(B_{j(i)} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{B(E)})\). * \(g_k + g_l\) are the random GCA effects of the parents, representing the additive portion of the family value. * \(s_{kl}\) is the random SCA effect of the cross, representing dominance deviations. * \((Eg)_{ik}\) and \((Eg)_{il}\) are the random interactions of the GCA of parents \(k\) and \(l\) with environment \(i\), modeling how additive breeding values change across environments: \((Eg) \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{GCA \times E})\). * \((Es)_{ikl}\) is the random interaction of the SCA of cross \(kl\) with environment \(i\), modeling the specific adaptability of a hybrid family to a particular environment: \((Es) \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{SCA \times E})\). * \(\epsilon_{ijklm}\) is the plot-level residual error, \(\epsilon \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{\epsilon})\).

4 Quantitative Genetics Interpretation

Using the variance components estimated from these models, Stelio can estimate key genetic parameters that describe the genetic architecture of phosphorus tolerance:

  1. Additive Genetic Variance (\(\sigma^2_A\)):
    • In a Diallel: \(\sigma^2_A = 4 \times \sigma^2_{GCA}\)
    • In a Factorial: \(\sigma^2_A = 2 \times (\sigma^2_F + \sigma^2_M)\) (or \(4 \times \sigma^2_{GCA\_pooled}\) if pooled)
  2. Dominance Genetic Variance (\(\sigma^2_D\)):
    • In both Diallel and Factorial: \(\sigma^2_D = 4 \times \sigma^2_{SCA}\) (or \(4 \times \sigma^2_{FM}\) in factorial)
  3. Narrow-Sense Heritability (\(h^2\)): Reflects the proportion of phenotypic variance due to additive genetic effects, which determine response to selection: \[h^2 = \frac{\sigma^2_A}{\sigma^2_{\text{phenotypic}}}\]
  4. Broad-Sense Heritability (\(H^2\)): Reflects the proportion of phenotypic variance due to total genetic effects (additive + dominance), useful if crosses can be vegetatively propagated or directly commercialized as \(F_1\) hybrids: \[H^2 = \frac{\sigma^2_A + \sigma^2_D}{\sigma^2_{\text{phenotypic}}}\]

# Linear Mixed Model Equations

To analyze these experiments, we must translate the physical design into statistical models. Below are the equations for both the Factorial Design (Stelio's setup) and the Diallel Design (simulated training setup), under single and multi-environment scenarios.

## Model 1: Factorial Mating (Line × Tester) Equations

In a Factorial mating design, the female and male lines are distinct. This means female general combining ability ($GCA_F$) and male general combining ability ($GCA_M$) are modeled as two separate, independent random effects with distinct variances.

### Single-Environment Model

$$y_{jpqk} = \mu + B_j + F_p + M_q + (FM)_{pq} + \epsilon_{jpqk}$$

Where:
*   $y_{jpqk}$ is the phenotype (yield) of the $k$-th plant of the cross between female $p$ and male $q$ in block $j$.
*   $\mu$ is the overall population mean.
*   $B_j$ is the random effect of the $j$-th block, assumed $B_j \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_B)$.
*   $F_p$ is the random GCA effect of the $p$-th female parent, assumed $F_p \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_F)$.
*   $M_q$ is the random GCA effect of the $q$-th male parent, assumed $M_q \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_M)$.
*   $(FM)_{pq}$ is the random SCA effect of the cross between female $p$ and male $q$, assumed $(FM)_{pq} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{FM})$.
*   $\epsilon_{jpqk}$ is the residual experimental error, assumed $\epsilon_{jpqk} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{\epsilon})$.

### Multi-Environment Model (Including Phosphorus Treatments)
When evaluating the factorial cross across multiple environments (e.g., two phosphorus treatments: low-P and normal-P):

$$y_{ijpqk} = \mu + E_i + B_{j(i)} + F_p + M_q + (FM)_{pq} + (EF)_{ip} + (EM)_{iq} + (EFM)_{ipq} + \epsilon_{ijpqk}$$

Where:
*   $E_i$ is the fixed effect of the $i$-th environment (phosphorus treatment).
*   $B_{j(i)}$ is the random effect of the $j$-th block nested within the $i$-th environment, $B_{j(i)} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{B(E)})$.
*   $(EF)_{ip}$ is the random interaction between the GCA of female $p$ and environment $i$, $(EF)_{ip} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{FE})$.
*   $(EM)_{iq}$ is the random interaction between the GCA of male $q$ and environment $i$, $(EM)_{iq} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{ME})$.
*   $(EFM)_{ipq}$ is the random interaction between the SCA of cross $pq$ and environment $i$ (SCA $\times$ E), $(EFM)_{ipq} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{FME})$.
*   $\epsilon_{ijpqk}$ is the residual error, $\epsilon_{ijpqk} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{\epsilon})$.

## Model 2: Diallel Mating (Overlaid Parents) Equations

In a Diallel mating design, the same parent can be used as a male or female. Therefore, the GCA effect of a parent must be identical whether it acts as the male or female. In mixed models, this is achieved by **overlaying the design matrices** of the female and male factors, resulting in a single GCA variance component ($\sigma^2_{GCA}$).

### Single-Environment Model
For a diallel cross evaluated in one environment under RCBD:

$$y_{jklm} = \mu + B_j + g_k + g_l + s_{kl} + r_{kl} + \epsilon_{jklm}$$

Where:
*   $y_{jklm}$ is the yield of the $m$-th individual in block $j$ from the cross between parents $k$ and $l$.
*   $g_k$ and $g_l$ are the random GCA effects of parent $k$ and parent $l$, respectively. They are constrained to be identically distributed: $g \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{GCA})$. The total additive genetic effect of the family is the sum of parental GCAs: $g_k + g_l$.
*   $s_{kl}$ is the random SCA effect of the cross between parents $k$ and $l$. It is symmetric ($s_{kl} = s_{lk}$), and $s_{kl} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{SCA})$.
*   $r_{kl}$ is the reciprocal effect ($r_{kl} = -r_{lk}$), modeling maternal or cytoplasmic effects.
*   $\epsilon_{jklm}$ is the random residual error, $\epsilon_{jklm} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{\epsilon})$.

### Multi-Environment Model (Simulation Standard)
For the 2-environment simulated training dataset, we model the diallel as:

$$y_{ijklm} = \mu + E_i + B_{j(i)} + g_k + g_l + s_{kl} + (Eg)_{ik} + (Eg)_{il} + (Es)_{ikl} + \epsilon_{ijklm}$$

Where:
*   $E_i$ is the fixed effect of environment $i$ ($i = \text{env1}, \text{env2}$).
*   $B_{j(i)}$ is the random block nested within environment, $B_{j(i)} \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{B(E)})$.
*   $g_k + g_l$ are the random GCA effects of the parents, representing the additive portion of the family value.
*   $s_{kl}$ is the random SCA effect of the cross, representing dominance deviations.
*   $(Eg)_{ik}$ and $(Eg)_{il}$ are the random interactions of the GCA of parents $k$ and $l$ with environment $i$, modeling how additive breeding values change across environments: $(Eg) \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{GCA \times E})$.
*   $(Es)_{ikl}$ is the random interaction of the SCA of cross $kl$ with environment $i$, modeling the specific adaptability of a hybrid family to a particular environment: $(Es) \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{SCA \times E})$.
*   $\epsilon_{ijklm}$ is the plot-level residual error, $\epsilon \sim \text{NID}(0, \sigma^2_{\epsilon})$.

# Quantitative Genetics Interpretation

Using the variance components estimated from these models, Stelio can estimate key genetic parameters that describe the genetic architecture of phosphorus tolerance:

1.  **Additive Genetic Variance ($\sigma^2_A$):**
    *   In a Diallel: $\sigma^2_A = 4 \times \sigma^2_{GCA}$
    *   In a Factorial: $\sigma^2_A = 2 \times (\sigma^2_F + \sigma^2_M)$ (or $4 \times \sigma^2_{GCA\_pooled}$ if pooled)
2.  **Dominance Genetic Variance ($\sigma^2_D$):**
    *   In both Diallel and Factorial: $\sigma^2_D = 4 \times \sigma^2_{SCA}$ (or $4 \times \sigma^2_{FM}$ in factorial)
3.  **Narrow-Sense Heritability ($h^2$):**
    Reflects the proportion of phenotypic variance due to additive genetic effects, which determine response to selection:
    $$h^2 = \frac{\sigma^2_A}{\sigma^2_{\text{phenotypic}}}$$
4.  **Broad-Sense Heritability ($H^2$):**
    Reflects the proportion of phenotypic variance due to total genetic effects (additive + dominance), useful if crosses can be vegetatively propagated or directly commercialized as $F_1$ hybrids:
    $$H^2 = \frac{\sigma^2_A + \sigma^2_D}{\sigma^2_{\text{phenotypic}}}$$
